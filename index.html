HISTORY, & Programs

The University of Arizona (U of A, or UArizona) is a public land-grant research university in Tucson, Arizona, founded in 1885. It was the first university established in the Arizona Territory, 27 years before Arizona became a state. The University of Arizona is one of three universities governed by the Arizona Board of Regents (the University of Arizona, Arizona State University, and Northern Arizona University).

Unique Programs

Tree-ring research: The university invented this field of study in 1937.

Space science: Has world-leading astronomy, planetary, and asteroid research, and played a key role in mapping the moon for the 1969 lunar landing.

Biosphere 2: A unique research facility dedicated to global scientific issues

Rankings

Earth Sciences is a top-ranked program, and it has strong programs in fields like Medicine, Rehabilitation Counseling, and Speech-Language Pathology, according to some reports.
