THE OLD-MAIN

Old Main is the University of Arizona's first building, completed in 1891, serving as classrooms, dorms, and library for the nascent university, symbolizing its enduring legacy despite periods of disrepair, military use during WWII, and major 2014 renovations that preserved its historic charm while modernizing it for today's university core, housing the President's office and museum.

  • Established: Groundbreaking for Old Main occurred in 1887, with the building completed in 1891, making it the first structure on the UA campus.
  • Early Functions: It housed all university functions: classrooms, library, offices, and dorms for the first faculty and 32 students.
  • Architectural Features: Designed with a large wrap-around porch for shade (passive cooling) and large windows for ventilation, a pioneering sustainable design.

    • Decline & Revival (1930s-1940s)

  • Condemned: By 1938, the building fell into disrepair and was condemned by Tucson.
